# Resource Taxonomy

Use these labels to keep the repository honest.

## Labels

### Core Topos

Use for resources that directly teach or use:

- elementary topoi;
- Grothendieck topoi;
- sheaves on sites;
- subobject classifiers;
- internal logic;
- geometric morphisms;
- classifying topoi.

Examples:

- textbooks on sheaves and topoi;
- lecture notes on elementary topos theory;
- papers proving theorems about topoi.

### Sheaf-Theoretic

Use for resources focused on sheaves, presheaves, local-to-global structure, or
cellular sheaves, even when they do not use full topos theory.

Examples:

- sheaves on graphs;
- sheaf-based data analysis;
- sheaf neural networks;
- contextuality via sheaves.

### Applied Category Theory

Use for categorical resources relevant to AI but not specifically topos theory.

Examples:

- monoidal categories;
- operads;
- lenses;
- coalgebras;
- Markov categories;
- categorical compositionality.

### AI-Relevant

Use when the resource has an explicit connection to:

- machine learning;
- neural networks;
- probabilistic reasoning;
- knowledge representation;
- automated reasoning;
- program semantics for AI systems;
- data integration or structured data analysis.

### Speculative Bridge

Use when the idea is plausible but not yet backed by a concrete model, theorem,
implementation, or widely recognized research program.

The label is not negative. It simply tells readers how much confidence to place
in the connection.

### Verify First

Use when a resource needs source checking before being treated as reliable.

Typical reasons:

- no DOI, arXiv link, publisher page, or author page;
- title sounds generated or cannot be found;
- institution or lab cannot be verified;
- claims are too broad for the evidence given;
- the item uses "topos" as a metaphor.

## Inclusion Checklist

Before adding an item, answer:

1. What is the actual source URL?
2. Who are the authors?
3. Is it about topoi, sheaves, or broader category theory?
4. What is the AI connection?
5. Is the connection direct, indirect, or speculative?
6. What should a learner read before this?

## Recommended Entry Format

```markdown
### Title

- Link: <https://example.org>
- Authors: Name, Name
- Labels: Core Topos, AI-Relevant
- Level: beginner | intermediate | advanced
- Why it matters: one or two precise sentences.
- Caution: optional note about limitations or scope.
```

## Removal Rules

Remove or quarantine an item when:

- the source cannot be verified;
- it is unrelated to topoi or sheaves;
- it duplicates a better entry;
- it makes inflated claims without evidence;
- it is primarily promotional.

Move uncertain items to a "Verify First" section rather than mixing them with
confirmed resources.
